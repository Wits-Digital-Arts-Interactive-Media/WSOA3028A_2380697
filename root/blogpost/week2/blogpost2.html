Exploring The Strucuture Of HTML

Discussing My Folder Structure, URL scheme and general decision-making

Folder Structure

I believe that this is essential for organizing and maintaining my project throughout its initial development stages and its active refinement

How have I decided to approach this?

I create 'root' folders that contain the main index.html
Thereafter, an additional folders are created to store the images, css and javascript files respectively.
These folders are easily accessible when referencing them as images or hyperlinks in the index.html
Additional folders are also created to store blogpost specific content, which will follow the same pattern of hierachial folder creation
I felt that this system allowed to keep track of information more efficiently,
and to distinguish between varying html links and their respective set of files

URL Scheme

I understand to a a way to navigate and locate resources on the web.
It provides a hierarchical and organized way to access different pages and assets within a website.

How am I approaching this?

I am utilzing my folder strucutre, that being a hierarchical file system for my website,
where the main page acts as the 'root' or 'trunk' of a tree.
This main page is then divided into different topics, each functioning as a 'branch' that further extends into more detailed content.
These individual topics serve as references or links to additional pages,
symbolizing the growth and expansion of the tree-like structure as users navigate through the various branches to access specific content on your website.
This approach provides a clear and organized way to present information,
allowing users to follow a logical path and explore different aspects of your site based on their interests or needs.

General Design Paramaters

This serves as my general thought-process and decision-making techniques whilst I am designing the website.

  • Consistency: I am sticking to the naming convention, and my aforementioned folder structure for ease of navigation.

  • Scalability: I am considering how user-friendly and practical my general design approach is so as my project grows, it is sustainable to maintain.

  • User-friendly URLs: I would like to create a website that is generally discernible, serving a functional purpose in an elegant format.

  • Research-based Development: I would like to refine my website based on continual learning,
    through research which reveals new knowledge, concept modules and varying techniques.

You Say You Want a Revolution?

What do I think the prime focus of this text is?

I think that this text primarily focuses on the web,
exploring the evolution and general impact of hypertext and media

What is the impact of hypertext and the web on our daily lives and society?

I belive that within the past 10 years especially,
in growing up and actively observing changes across the web,
it has had a profound and transformative impact in the following ways:

  • Information Accessibility:
    I feel that the web has significantly increased our ability
    to both retrieve and share vast amounts of information on a diverse range of topics.
    This has expanded our resources within education, research and general insight on contemporary news.
    This ranges from more scientifically accurate and researched medical journals,
    to casual blogposts and recipe websites.
  • Communication and Connectivity:
    I appreciate how much our virtual communication has evolved from emails,
    to social media platforms that are able to facilitate communication real-time interactions.
    Resultantly, this has not only eased our ability to readily/conveniently communicate with friends and family,
    but fosters a sense of global interconnectedness.
  • Democratization:
    Platforms like reddit allow individuals with an interest to partipicate in various discussions,
    for which they can freely share ideas and even publish content for public/private acknowledgement.
    This has created an immersive forum for people to learn more information and express themselves in an easily accessible way.
    Additionally, empowering individuals and marginalized voices that may not have had a platform in traditional media.
  • Cultural and Social Changes:
    I feel social media platforms have become so prevelant within my generation.
    and hence plays a significant role in shaping cultural trends and societal norms.
    They provide a platform for various kinds of self-expression, activism and cultrural exchange
    - which I feel has promoted diversity and inclusion within society.
    However, there has been many concerns associated with mental health, privacy and widespread of misinformation.
    I have been cyberbullied in the past about my weight/appearance,
    which was worsened by idealistic views of 'body image'
    and 'diet culture' by social media influencers.
  • Education and Learning:
    I can personally attest to how the web has transformed by educational experience.
    I now have access to a stupendous number of resources in the form of educational websites (Chegg, Homeworkify),
    alongside online courses (Udemy) which have supplemented by university lecturers.
    I am able to learn and advance my understanding of concepts at my own pace to avoid feeling overwhelmed during in-person lecturers.

Considering McLuhan's idea of a medium reversing when taken to its limit
, what do I believe the Web becomes at its extreme?

I believe this idea suggests that when a medium is taken to its 'extreme' or reaches its perceived limit,
it can have adverse effects that stray from its intention.

How does this manifest?

  • Information Overload:
     I feel like the sheer volume of data and content across the web can be
    quite overwhelming or challenging for users to ascertain the information
    that is most relevant, accurate and meaningful to them.
    There is simply too much to navigate through!
  • Loss Of Depth & Context:
     I feel as though short-form content, rapid scolling and quick consumption patterns through algorithms
    (such as YouTube shorts, tiktoks and instagram reels) could lead to an oversimplication of complex subjects.
    It sort of creates the 'broken telephone' problem,
    where nuances derived from its historical context is overlooked.
  • Erosion Of Privacy:
     I feel as though my privacy is labelled as a 'priority' when in actuality there is
    constant surveillance, data collection and the commodification of my personal information
    across social media platforms (such as Facebook).
    The mere fact that there are court-cases filed,
    such as the 2018 Facebook debacle is very telling of the societal concerns and ethical issues that we are currently facing.
    Are we desensitized to our basic right to privacy?
  • Echo Chambers:
     I feel like because of existing algorithms that curate content based on our preferences,
    it often reinforces echo chambers and polarization.
    Hence, we have limited exposure to varying perspectives,
    which I think intrinsically promotes entrenched beliefs and instrumentally creates greater divides within society.
